Research

  • High-z galaxies: Dusty star-forming galaxies; Galaxy Clustering; COSMOS; Lensing

My research lies in the area of observational astrophysics and galaxy evolution. I focus on two major topics: (1) dusty star-forming galaxies and (2) clusters of galaxies at high redshift, aiming to reveal their formation, growth, and evolution across the history of the Universe. I'm most interested in searching for the most distant dusty galaxies and protoclusters, aim to pioneer our view of galaxy and structure formation. Nowadays, I expand my field to gravitational lensing and quiescent galaxies at high redshifts.

For my research, I use multi-wavelength data ranging from optical to radio. I specialise in infrared (JWST, HST, VLT, VISTA, Spitzer), submm/mm (ALMA, NOEMA, Herschel, JCMT, IRAM), and radio observations (VLA, MeerKAT) in the COSMOS field. Some selected publications are listed below:
